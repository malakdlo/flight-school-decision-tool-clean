# Tulsa Riverside Airport (KRVS) Radio Guide

> Source of truth for the radio call script builder's towered templates.
> Supplied by the owner (from their actual KRVS training script) on 2026-07-10.
> KRVS is a Class D field under the Tulsa Class C shelf — departures and arrivals
> use Tulsa Departure/Approach for radar service even though we don't fly into Class C.

## General Radio Reminder

When you **initiate** a call, say:

1. Who you are calling
2. Your callsign

> "Riverside Ground, Cessna _____."

When you are **responding** to a call, repeat the instruction and say your callsign last.

> "Taxi to Runway 19R via Alpha, Cessna 8136F."

---

# Outbound from Tulsa Riverside

## 1. Obtain ATIS

After engine start, obtain the current ATIS information on **126.5**.

Note the phonetic letter associated with the current information:

- Information Bravo
- Information November
- Information Echo
- Etc.

---

## 2. Contact Ground or Clearance Delivery

When ready to taxi, contact:

- **Riverside Ground: 121.7**
- Or, rarely, **Clearance Delivery: 124.5**, when specified by ATIS

### Local Maneuver Flight

> "Riverside Ground, Cessna _____ at PlaneBros with information _____, requesting radar service to the west."

Or:

> "Riverside Ground, Cessna _____ at PlaneBros with information _____, requesting radar service to Pogue."

If ATIS directs you to Clearance Delivery:

> "Riverside Clearance, Cessna _____ at PlaneBros with information _____, requesting radar service to the west."

### Cross-Country Flight

> "Riverside Ground, Cessna _____ at PlaneBros with information _____, requesting flight following to _____."

Use the destination airport identifier in the blank.

### Expected Ground Response

Riverside Ground may respond:

> "Cessna _____, maintain VFR at or below 2,500, Tulsa Departure frequency is 124.0, squawk _____."

Or:

> "Cessna _____, maintain VFR at or below 2,500, Tulsa Departure frequency is 134.7, squawk _____."

Repeat back:

- The altitude restriction
- The Departure frequency
- The squawk code

Ground may then say:

> "Cessna _____, readback correct, taxi to Runway 19R via Alpha, cross Runway 13."

Or:

> "Cessna _____, taxi to Runway 1L via Alpha."

Repeat the taxi instructions and taxi to the appropriate run-up area.

---

## 3. Complete the Run-Up

When you have finished the run-up checks and are ready to taxi to the runway:

> "Riverside Ground, Cessna _____, run-up complete."

Ground may respond:

> "Cessna _____, number _____, contact Tower when number one."

Repeat the instruction and taxi to the runway hold-short line.

Contact Tower on **120.3** once you are number one for takeoff.

---

## 4. Contact Riverside Tower

> "Riverside Tower, Cessna _____, number one at Runway _____ for departure to _____."

Tower may issue one of several instructions.

### Hold Short

> "Cessna _____, hold short of Runway 19R for landing traffic."

### Line Up and Wait

> "Cessna _____, Runway 19R, line up and wait."

### Cleared for Takeoff

> "Cessna _____, fly heading 230, Runway _____, cleared for takeoff."

Repeat the instruction verbatim, including the runway number, and execute the clearance.

---

## 5. Contact Tulsa Departure

After takeoff, Riverside Tower will eventually say something such as:

> "Cessna _____, turn right heading _____, contact Tulsa Departure."

Repeat the instruction and switch to the assigned Tulsa Departure frequency.

### Initial Departure Check-In

> "Tulsa Departure, Cessna _____, off Riverside, climbing through _____ for 2,500, heading _____."

Tulsa Departure may respond:

> "Cessna _____, radar contact, continue Tower-assigned heading."

Or:

> "Cessna _____, radar contact, climb on course to the west at or below 4,500."

Repeat and follow the instructions.

After any temporary altitude or heading restriction, Tulsa Departure will eventually release you to fly toward the area or destination you originally requested, such as:

- West practice area
- Pogue
- Bristow
- Cross-country destination

### Important

A new heading clearance is not automatically an altitude clearance, and a new altitude clearance is not automatically a heading clearance.

You remain restricted to your original altitude ceiling of **2,500 feet** until Tulsa Departure assigns or approves a different altitude.

---

## 6. Radar-Service Termination

For a local flight, once you are sufficiently far from Tulsa's terminal area, Departure may say:

> "Cessna _____, radar service terminated, squawk VFR, frequency change approved."

Repeat the instruction and change the transponder code to **1200**.

You are then no longer receiving radar service and may change frequencies.

### Cross-Country Flight Following

For a cross-country flight with flight following, Tulsa Departure may eventually hand you to another controller:

> "Cessna _____, contact Kansas City Center on 128.6."

The specific facility and frequency will depend on your route.

Repeat the frequency and switch to the assigned controller.

# Returning to Tulsa Riverside

This script represents a typical arrival, but there may be adjustments or exceptions because of:

- Traffic
- Weather
- Runway configuration
- Controller workload
- Other operating conditions

Most radio work consists of listening for your callsign, repeating instructions, and following them.

You can always say:

> "Say again."

Never pretend to understand an instruction or clearance that you do not understand.

---

## 1. Obtain Riverside ATIS

After a maneuver flight or pattern work at another local airport, climb high enough to receive Riverside ATIS on **126.5**.

If you cannot receive ATIS, you may still be too far away or too low.

You can often begin receiving it around **2,500 feet**.

Record:

- The current ATIS information letter
- The active runway information
- The current Tulsa Approach frequency, normally 124.0 or 134.7

---

## 2. Contact Tulsa Approach

Contact Tulsa Approach and provide:

1. Who you are calling
2. Your callsign
3. Your location
4. Your altitude
5. Your intention to return to Riverside
6. The current ATIS information

### Example

> "Tulsa Approach, Cessna _____, five miles south of Keystone Dam at 3,000, inbound Riverside with information _____."

Tulsa Approach may respond after several seconds:

> "Cessna _____, Tulsa Approach, squawk _____."

Repeat the squawk code and enter it into the transponder.

---

## 3. Follow Tulsa Approach Instructions

Tulsa Approach may say:

> "Cessna _____, radar contact, six miles south of Keystone Dam, proceed direct Riverside, expect Runway 19R."

Or:

> "Cessna _____, radar contact, proceed direct Riverside, expect Runway 1L."

Other possible instructions include:

> "Fly to the Turnpike Split, expect Runway 19R."

Or:

> "Fly direct Turkey Mountain, expect Runway 19R."

Tulsa Approach can see the traffic situation at Riverside and may maneuver you to create spacing between you and other aircraft before handing you to Riverside Tower.

Repeat and execute the instructions.

---

## 4. Contact Riverside Tower

Approximately four to five miles from Riverside, Tulsa Approach may say:

> "Cessna _____, contact Riverside Tower on 120.3."

Repeat the frequency, switch to Tower, and make your initial call.

Do not forget to complete the frequency change.

### Full-Stop Landing

> "Riverside Tower, Cessna _____, inbound for a full stop."

### Requesting Pattern Work

> "Riverside Tower, Cessna _____, inbound, requesting _____ laps in the pattern."

---

## 5. Enter the Traffic Pattern

Tower may issue one of several pattern-entry instructions.

### Right Base

> "Cessna _____, enter a right base for Runway 19R."

### Midfield Right Downwind

> "Cessna _____, enter a midfield right downwind for Runway 19R."

### Midfield Left Downwind

> "Cessna _____, enter a midfield left downwind for Runway 1L."

Repeat the pattern-entry instruction and follow it.

---

## 6. Additional Sequencing Instructions

When the airport is busy, Tower may say:

> "I'll call your base."

This means you may enter and continue on the assigned downwind, but you must not turn base until Tower instructs you to do so.

Tower may also ask you to follow another aircraft:

> "Follow an Archer on left downwind. Report traffic in sight."

Repeat the instruction and look for the traffic.

Once you see it:

> "Traffic in sight, Cessna _____."

If you do not understand an instruction:

> "Say again, Cessna _____."

Never pretend to understand a clearance that you do not understand.

---

## 7. Landing Clearance

Tower may eventually say:

> "Cessna _____, number one, Runway 19R, cleared for the option."

Or:

> "Cessna _____, number two, Runway 19R, cleared for the option."

For a full-stop landing, Tower may say:

> "Cessna _____, number one, Runway 19R, cleared to land."

Or:

> "Cessna _____, number two, Runway 19R, cleared to land."

The busier the airport is, the later the landing clearance may be issued.

Repeat the landing clearance, including the runway number.

---

## Cleared for the Option

"Cleared for the option" is normally used when you are remaining in the pattern.

It permits several options, including:

- Touch-and-go
- Stop-and-go
- Low approach
- Full-stop landing
- Full-stop taxi-back

"Cleared to land" is normally issued when you have stated that the landing will be your final landing.

---

## 8. Exit the Runway

After the final landing, Tower may issue one of several instructions.

### Exit When Able

> "Cessna _____, turn right when able, contact Ground."

### Exit onto Runway 31

> "Cessna _____, turn right on Runway 31, contact Ground."

### Exit onto Taxiway Alpha 3

> "Cessna _____, turn right on Alpha 3, contact Ground."

### Taxi to Parking on Tower Frequency

> "Cessna _____, turn right on Runway 31, taxi to parking this frequency."

This means you should remain on the Tower frequency while taxiing to parking.

This may happen when the airport is quiet or when a separate Ground controller is not operating.

Repeat the instruction, exit the runway, and complete the after-landing checklist.

Do not stop on the runway to complete the checklist.

---

## 9. Contact Riverside Ground

Unless Tower has instructed you to taxi to parking on the Tower frequency, contact Riverside Ground on **121.7** after exiting the runway.

> "Riverside Ground, Cessna _____ at _____, request taxi to PlaneBros."

Ground may respond:

> "Cessna _____, taxi to PlaneBros via Alpha."

Repeat the taxi instruction and taxi to parking.

---

# Quick Outbound Flow

**ATIS → Ground or Clearance → Run-up → Ground → Tower → Tulsa Departure**

1. Obtain ATIS on 126.5.
2. Contact Ground on 121.7 or Clearance on 124.5 when directed.
3. Request radar service or flight following.
4. Copy the altitude restriction, Departure frequency, and squawk code.
5. Taxi to the run-up area.
6. Report run-up complete.
7. Contact Tower on 120.3 when number one.
8. Depart according to Tower's clearance.
9. Contact Tulsa Departure when instructed.
10. Remain within all assigned heading and altitude restrictions.

---

# Quick Arrival Flow

**Riverside ATIS → Tulsa Approach → Riverside Tower → Riverside Ground**

1. Obtain Riverside ATIS on 126.5.
2. Contact the appropriate Tulsa Approach frequency.
3. Provide your location, altitude, destination, and ATIS code.
4. Enter the assigned squawk code.
5. Follow Approach's routing and altitude instructions.
6. Contact Riverside Tower on 120.3 when instructed.
7. Enter the assigned traffic-pattern leg.
8. Follow sequencing instructions.
9. Receive and repeat the landing clearance.
10. Exit the runway as instructed.
11. Contact Ground on 121.7 unless told to remain with Tower.
12. Taxi to PlaneBros or the assigned parking area.
